Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • What is a loan application?

    This application is required and necessary when a you apply for a mortgage. It has all the necessary information a lender needs in order to determine if you are credit worthy and what types of credit terms you will have like the interest rate, minimum down payment requirements, possible amortization terms.

    All mortgage applications are accompanied by disclosures and are necessary to have a complete loan package.

    In this digital era, most loan applications are completed digitally and signed on a computer or on a smart phone. You can complete a physical application but digital documentation makes for a faster and easier process for you as the borrower.

  • What are the steps to close on a loan?
    • 1.Loan application is completed (1003)
    • 2.Borrower is prequalified
    • 3.Borrower has an executed contract for a home
    • 4.Initial loan documents are obtained
    • 5.Loan is submitted to underwriting for approval
    • 6.An underwriting loan approval is received and all conditions are sent in order to get a clear-to-close
    • 7.Borrower signs all closing documents and now owns the home and has a mortgage
    • * An underwriter is a person representing the lender who reviews and approves or denies a home loan. Clear-to-close means there are no more documents or steps needed to close the loan and closing date can be scheduled.
  • What are digital loans and why are they better?

    Mortgage loans can be done completely paperless now. From the pre-qualification process to the application, and now even the closing. Closings can be done via computer with a notary explaining the all closing documents and verifying identity. Everything has changed and become much faster and easier for both lenders and borrowers. Take advantage of our digital capabilities and learn as much as possible to how the process works and what needs to be done. This is a major advantage when competing for a home as you will be able to provide pre-approval letters and loan approvals in hours not days or weeks.

  • What is a pre-approval letter?

    A preapproval letter is a letter that the mortgage loan officer or bank representative provides a borrower and/or realtor, in order to show that the borrower has the capacity to purchase that home at that price as long as they are able to provide all the necessary documents requested by the bank.

    This is an important letter when trying to beat other buyer's on a home that’s for sale.
